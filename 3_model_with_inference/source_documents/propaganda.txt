1 Appeal to Authority
Definition: Supposes that a claim is true because a valid authority or expert on the issue supports it.
Example: "The World Health Organization stated that this new vaccine is the most effective treatment available, and doctors around the globe recommend it."

2 Appeal to Fear/Prejudice
Definition: Builds support for an idea by instilling anxiety and/or panic in the audience towards an alternative.
Example: "If we don't enact these new security measures, we will be at greater risk of terrorist attacks, jeopardizing our safety."

3 Bandwagon, Reductio ad Hitlerum
Definition: Justify actions or ideas because everyone else is doing it, or reject them because it’s favored by groups despised by the target audience.
Example: "Everyone is switching to this new social media platform; don't be left out!" or "This policy is favored by extremist groups; we should reject it."

4 Black-and-White Fallacy
Definition: Presents two alternative options as the only possibilities, when actually more options exist.
Example: "You must either support this law, or you are against public safety."

5 Causal Oversimplification
Definition: Assumes a single reason for an issue when there are multiple causes.
Example: "The economic downturn is solely due to immigration policies."

6 Doubt
Definition: Questioning the credibility of someone or something.
Example: "Can we really trust her to handle our finances after her previous business failed?"

7 Exaggeration, Minimization
Definition: Either representing something in an excessive manner or making something seem less important than it actually is.
Example: "He single-handedly revolutionized the industry!" or "It was just a minor mistake, nothing significant."

8 Flag-Waving
Definition: Playing on strong national feeling (or with respect to a group, e.g., race, gender, political preference) to justify or promote an action or idea.
Example: "Supporting this bill is a patriotic duty for every citizen who loves their country."

9 Loaded Language
Definition: Uses specific phrases and words that carry strong emotional impact to affect the audience.
Example: "The ruthless corporation is laying off thousands of dedicated employees."

10 Name Calling/Labeling
Definition: Gives a label to the object of the propaganda campaign as either the audience hates or loves.
Example: "Only an idiot would fall for such obvious lies."

11 Repetition
Definition: Repeats the message over and over in the article so that the audience will accept it.
Example: "Our leader's wisdom is unmatched. Their decisions are always fair and just."

12 Slogans
Definition: A brief and striking phrase that contains labeling and stereotyping.
Example: "Build back better!"

13 Thought-Terminating Clichés
Definition: Words or phrases that discourage critical thought and useful discussion about a given topic.
Example: "That's just the way it is."

14 Whataboutism, Straw Men, Red Herring
Definition: Attempts to discredit an opponent’s position by charging them with hypocrisy without directly disproving their argument.
Example: "How can you criticize this administration's policy when your own party had similar issues?"
